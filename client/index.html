Rule Definitions

Source: Examination & Assessment Regs: Boards of Examiners

Rule A
The classification of the weighted mean of all relevant credits at Level 5 and all relevant credits at Level 6 in the ratio of 40:60 respectively after first discounting the marks in the worst 20 credits both at Level 5 and at Level 6.
Rule B
The classification of the weighted mean of all relevant credits at Level 6 after first discounting the marks in the worst 20 credits at Level 6.
Rule C
The minimum classification in which more than 50% of the combined relevant credits at Level 5 and Level 6 were attained after first discounting the marks in the worst 20 credits both at Level 5 and at Level 6.

Weighted mean averages are rounded to integers when applying these rules, as required in section 3.2 of the above regulations.

About & Explanation

Welcome to our webapp designed to make your academic journey a little easier. Our app is designed to help you calculate your final degree classification and GPA based on your module marks. You can set a target final degree classification, and it will calculate the minimum grades you need to achieve that goal based on the University's marking rules that are outlined above.

By inputting your UP number, you can have your module grades automatically imported.

The indication field shows the classification of the grade itself and if switched to Calculate Target Grade, then it will give you the option of what final predicted grade the student wants to archive.

Modules are highlighted that need more attention to help you focus on improving your grades to achieve a target degree classification.

GPA stands for "Grade Point Average." It is a numerical representation of a student's academic performance, calculated by averaging the grades they have received in their courses. The University of Portsmouth is part of an experiment introducing GPA in UK universities; as outlined here Advance HE - Grade Point Average (GPA).

If you are a student that entered at the Final Year, select the checkbox for Final Year Entry Student. This will allow the calculations to ignore the second year modules, and only use the final year modules in its calculations. (Following rule B only.)
